Brief Description

The American pawpaw is a small, deciduous tree native to North America. It produces greenish-yellow fruits with a custard-like texture and sweet, tropical flavor reminiscent of banana and mango. The pawpaw is often referred to as the "poor man's banana" and is prized for its unique taste.

Harvest times: Late summer to early fall.

Taxonomy

Family: Annonaceae

Genus: Asimina

Species: A. triloba

Types

The American pawpaw (Asimina triloba) is the most common species of pawpaw cultivated for its fruit. While there are no distinct "types" of American pawpaw, variations in flavor and fruit size may occur based on genetics and growing conditions.
