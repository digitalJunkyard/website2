Brief Description

The prickly pear, also known as Opuntia, is a genus of cacti native to the Americas. It is characterized by flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and produces colorful, edible fruits known as prickly pears. Prickly pears are prized for their sweet taste and are used in various culinary dishes and beverages.

Harvest times: Late summer to early fall.

Taxonomy

Family: Cactaceae

Genus: Opuntia

Species: Various species within the Opuntia genus

Types

There are numerous species and varieties of prickly pear, each with its own unique characteristics and flavors.

Some common species include:

  • Opuntia engelmannii (Engelmann prickly pear)
  • Opuntia ficus-indica (Indian fig opuntia)
  • Opuntia humifusa (Eastern prickly pear)
