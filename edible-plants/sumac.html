Brief Description

Sumac is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae. The sumac species are shrubs and small trees that produce clusters of red, hairy fruits known as drupes. Some species of sumac are edible and have been used for culinary and medicinal purposes for centuries.

Harvest times: Late summer to early fall.

Taxonomy

Family: Anacardiaceae

Genus: Rhus

Species: Various species within the Rhus genus

Edible Sumac Types

Some common edible species of sumac include:

  • Rhus typhina (Staghorn sumac)
  • Rhus glabra (Smooth sumac)
  • Rhus coriaria (Tanner's sumac)
