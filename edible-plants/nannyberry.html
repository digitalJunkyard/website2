Brief Description

The nannyberry, also known as Viburnum lentago, is a species of viburnum native to North America. It is a large deciduous shrub or small tree that produces clusters of small, edible berries in late summer to early fall. Nannyberries are prized for their sweet taste and versatility in cooking.

Harvest times: Late summer to early fall.

Taxonomy

Family: Adoxaceae

Genus: Viburnum

Species: V. lentago

Types

The nannyberry (Viburnum lentago) is the only species within the Viburnum genus commonly referred to as nannyberry.

However, variations in fruit size and flavor may occur based on genetics and growing conditions.
