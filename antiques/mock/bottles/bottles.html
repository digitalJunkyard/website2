Preparing the Bottles

I bought the bottles (actual late 1800's bottles) at an antique store outside of Columbia at $6 each. There were a lot of them. I picked the ones that looked easier to clean out.

Then I went to Lowes and got some corks. These are not composite corks. To make them look aged, sand them down all over, soften the edges, and then rub in moist coffee grounds. Gently rinse off.

For my large lotion bottles, I bought large brown lotion bottles on amazon. Too bad the pump is always plastic.

Printing and Preparing

  • First, I printed out the labels. You can see the bottles I've already done in the background.
  • Then I put some coffee grounds and a little warm or hot water in a mug.
  • I used my finger to spread on the moist coffee grounds and dampen/wet the paper.
  • I rubbed dry-ish coffee grounds in sporadic places.
  • I sanded the edges with wet coffee grounds.
  • I let it dry for 30 seconds to a minute, and then peeled it off the counter with my nail. Then I placed it back onto the counter.
  • Then maybe a minute later, but while still wet, I used school glue to glue it. I made sure to get out the bubbles from within, and glue the entire part of the bottle. I even made sure to get glue on the front of the paper, so if the bottle gets wet, it wont comprimise the label.
