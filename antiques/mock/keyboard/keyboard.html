Styling a Keyboard as a Typewriter

Click photos to expand!

Buting the new keyboard

I bought a basic, inexpensive keyboard on Amazon thatc had the look I wanted. The buttons were black and round, and the base was a dark lovely green color. The listing is here.

Creating the stickers

I found a basic sheet of keyboard stickers online, and had to use Gemini to make them look like antique typewriter keys. Unfortunately, this isn't something you can find free online. I went back and forth with Gemini many times. It was enraging. I finally got it close enough where I could use GIMP to correct everything.
I printed them all out on sticker paper, stuck them all on, and then painted on polyurethane for brightness and durability.

Creating a Base

I wanted a nice slanted base to give it a typewriter look. I didn't want to make it totally accurate because it would take up a lot of desk space. The slant is also very helpful because I recline in my chair and its hard to reach the keys. I can't push in my chair because my rolltop desk isn't tall enough, which puts distance between me and the keys.

I decided to make it out of packing polystyrene. I used a cardboard knife and hot glue to make it. I made the side details out of a squishy packing foam.

Painting the Mount

I used acrylic craft paint. I mixed a little bit of forest green into black paint. Everything was painted that color except for the gold details. I used my gold paint for that. I have a darker irridescent bronze paint that I used for the 'screws' of the tab things. Those are actually just drops of hot glue. Then I painted over everything with polyurethane for durability.

Putting it all together

I placed my keyboard into the mount and it works perfectly!
