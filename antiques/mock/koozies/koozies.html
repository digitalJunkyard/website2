Quilted Koozies

Click photos to expand!

Process

I don't know if these are reall considered mock-antiques, but the bottles are vintage and I thought I'd share this cool project I did.
  • I bought felt at walmart and had to extend some of the sheets of felt.
  • I used poly-fill quilt batting.
  • I drew up some sketches with ideas of the design.
  • Basically, I just quilted from there how the pictures would suggest. I used my imagingation with some fun waves and patterns.
  • I cut out other pieces of felt to make more colors.
  • I drew with washable markers how I wanted things embroidered. I got the old Pepsi-Cola logo online. The font for that actually pre-dates the classic blue and red bottle cap design logo.
  • I added more embroidery, pre-programmed stitches, etc.
  • Everything was free-hand machine embroidered. Free-motion embroidered process. Except I dont use an embroidery foot, just a zigzag stitch. I just cant get an embroidery foot to work.
  • I measured everything out by hand. I made the bottom circles.
  • I sewed these together, making a full koozie, but I still wanted the top part to protect the upper half.
  • I did the same quilting process with the upper half. Except before I did the quilted wave pattern, I cut holes in one side of the felt and ran thick yarn though it, in order to cinch it.
    • These top halves were too small, so I decided to create patch things to bridge the gap. I made them red and blue, and embroidered the logo text again onto those and sewed everything up.
